A $100,000 boutique studio project appears at $400,000 to $700,000 at a large agency. Same deliverable. The delta pays for account management and overhead, not engineering quality.

We documented actual pricing ranges for custom software in 2026, broken down by what you're building and who you're hiring.

The ranges are wide because the work is genuinely different. A focused internal tool starts at $15,000. A full product build with multi-tenancy and real integrations runs $100,000 to $400,000 at boutique rates. Both are technically custom software. The difference is scope.

Offshore development is often assumed to be significantly cheaper. On well-scoped projects, total cost difference closes to within 20 to 30 percent of boutique studio rates. Lower hourly rates come with higher revision counts and more communication overhead. On poorly scoped projects, offshore frequently ends up more expensive in total.

Requirements quality matters more than buyers typically budget for. A discovery phase costs $5,000 to $20,000. It almost always saves more than that over the build. The cost of a vague brief shows up as revision cycles and scope disputes, not as a transparent line item on the original proposal.

- Annual maintenance: 15 to 20 percent of initial build cost per year

One more number worth knowing: rush work adds 20 to 40 percent on top of the standard rate at most studios.

If you are evaluating a software quote right now, the most useful question is whether you are comparing studios at the same market tier. A boutique quote and a big-agency quote are not comparable documents. Comparing them directly produces confusion, not clarity.
