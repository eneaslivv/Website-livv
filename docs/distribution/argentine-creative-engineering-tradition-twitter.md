1/ Walk through Stripe, Linear, Notion, or Vercel and you will find at least one Argentine on the team. Often the head of design. Often the engineer who decided the typography. This is not coincidence. ↓

2/ Auth0, founded by two Argentines, was acquired by Okta for $6.5B in 2021. Etermax shipped Trivia Crack to over one billion downloads, from Buenos Aires. Globant builds for some of the best-known American brands. The list goes on.

3/ The conditions run forty years deep. FADU-UBA's design program opened in 1985 with a lineage from Tomás Maldonado, who shaped postwar European design at Germany's HfG Ulm. Rubén Fontana taught the next generation's typographers.

4/ The 2001 Argentine economic crisis broke the conventional career path. An entire generation went freelance, learned to bill in dollars, and sold work to US and European clients. The pattern repeated in 2018 and again in 2024.

5/ Buenos Aires shares full working-hour overlap with US East Coast hours. The largest brand agencies in New York and London have a Buenos Aires studio on retainer whose name they will not say out loud.

6/ The craft fingerprint: hierarchy obsession, restraint over spectacle, motion fluency, and typography literacy at the level of a print art director. The work steps out of the way rather than arguing with the user.

7/ The tradition has never been named in English. We wrote it because we have never found a version of this explanation online that we trusted.
