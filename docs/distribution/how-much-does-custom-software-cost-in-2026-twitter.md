1/ A $100k boutique software project appears at $400k to $700k at a large agency. Same deliverable. The premium covers account management and overhead, not craft quality. Here's what custom software actually costs in 2026. ↓

2/ The market has three tiers. Boutique studios: $100 to $200/hr. Mid-tier agencies: $150 to $300/hr. Large agencies: $250 to $600/hr. The cost difference above boutique reflects team size and process overhead, not meaningfully different engineering.

3/ By project type at boutique studio rates:

Marketing site: $20k to $80k
Web app MVP: $50k to $150k
Full product: $100k to $400k
Internal tool: $15k to $25k

All ranges assume stable requirements.

4/ AI adds a distinct line item. A single AI feature via model API: $8k to $40k. A RAG knowledge system: $20k to $80k. A custom AI agent: $30k to $120k. Building AI in from the start adds 10 to 20% to base cost.

5/ Offshore development runs $20 to $80/hr. But on well-scoped projects, total cost closes to within 20 to 30% of boutique rates. Revision counts are higher. Communication overhead is higher. The savings are smaller than the hourly gap suggests.

6/ The biggest cost variable is requirements quality, not hourly rate. A discovery phase costs $5k to $20k. It almost always saves more than that over the build. Rush work adds 20 to 40% on top of the standard rate.
