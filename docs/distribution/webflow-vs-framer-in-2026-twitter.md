1/ We shipped a Webflow site and a Framer site in the same quarter. Most comparisons are written by people who have used one seriously. Here is what actually differs. ↓

2/ Webflow wins on multi-locale at scale. We rebuilt a franchise site (UK + US, structurally divergent content trees, custom locale switcher) for this specifically. Framer can do localization. That case would have meant fighting the tool.

3/ Webflow wins on deep CMS. 200+ items, nested two to three layers deep, no performance loss. Framer has caught up substantially. For 100+ items with complex reference relationships, Webflow is still the safer pick.

4/ Framer wins on motion. The Mobilita site: scroll-velocity parallax, cursor-speed hover states, viewport-position deceleration. All configuration in Framer. In Webflow, each of those is custom code.

5/ Framer wins on designer onboarding. A senior designer who has never opened Framer ships a polished page in two days. Webflow takes a week. Framer's defaults are also closer to good before any custom work starts.

6/ Our default at LIVV: Framer for new marketing sites. It shifts to Webflow on three specific triggers: multi-locale at scale, 100+ CMS items with deep references, or enterprise governance. When a trigger fires, Webflow. No hesitation.
