1/ There are four pricing models for white-label web design. Two survive long-term. Two collapse before the third project. Most agencies encounter the wrong two first. ↓

2/ The commodity tier runs $2k-$6k/month per agency: subscription-based, section-priced, async delivery from a shared pool. Output is usually fine. It is not what makes a client renew.

3/ The serious tier prices by engagement, not by section. It sells through referrals. It does not advertise. Six characteristics: senior-only delivery, embedded process, clear ownership, transparent pricing, brand discipline, and enough time to build shared judgement.

4/ The agency economics: on a $120k project, internal delivery costs ~$80k. A white-label partner costs ~$50k. Agency margin: $40k internally vs $70k through white-label. Both sides make their number.

5/ The failure mode that ends most white-label engagements: the relationship was priced like commodity work but expected to deliver like a partner. That gap shows up in the third project, not the first.

6/ Retainer rates at the serious tier: $15k-$30k/month for one senior, $30k-$60k for two. Fixed-fee marketing sites run $20k-$75k. Studio partner rates sit 30-40% below direct-client rates.
