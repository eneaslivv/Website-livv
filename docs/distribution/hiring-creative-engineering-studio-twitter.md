1/ Most hiring guides are written by buyers. This one is written by the studio. After two years of sales calls, bids won, and bids lost, here is what we wish buyers knew. ↓

2/ A creative engineering studio is not a marketing agency with engineering added. Design and development run through the same team. The work that ships in the browser is the work the designer drew. No handoff loss.

3/ You probably need one if: budget $30k–$300k, project needs both visual craft and production engineering, no senior in-house design and engineering already. Under $15k? Hire a senior freelancer instead.

4/ Real 2026 boutique rates:
- Marketing site: $20k–$80k
- Product MVP: $50k–$200k
- Design system: $30k–$100k
- Mobile app MVP: $60k–$200k
Mid-tier agencies charge 2–3x. Big agencies charge 5–10x. The craft difference does not match the multiple.

5/ Red flags that matter during sales:
- Founder pitches, someone else runs your project
- Hourly pricing with no cap
- Portfolio mostly older than 18 months
- Cannot explain a decision in detail, only the result

6/ The most useful signal: does the studio push back? Senior studios tell you the scope is too big, the brief is unclear. A studio that agrees to everything in the first call is selling, not partnering.
