1/ Most founders ask "should we build or buy?" as one question about the whole stack.

That framing is the source of most bad decisions in this category. ↓

2/ The useful version of the question is specific:

Which one tool produces the most measurable operational cost, and does the 36-month math justify replacing it with a custom alternative?

That version is answerable with evidence.

3/ SaaS pricing pages hide the real cost.

A 20-person team on a $15/seat project management tool can pay $28,000-$35,000 over 36 months. The pricing page implied $13,000 at signup.

For mid-market tools, 36-month TCO runs 2-4x the initial number.

4/ Integration middleware is its own cost center.

12 SaaS tools with 20 active connections: $29,000-$72,000 over 36 months in subscription costs alone, before factoring in staff time to maintain them.

5/ Custom software is front-loaded, not subscription-based.

A focused workflow app at a boutique studio: $30,000-$100,000 to build, $1,000-$3,000/month to maintain.

The math shifts at 50+ seats. Below 15 people, SaaS almost always wins.

6/ Custom earns its cost in four situations:

- The workflow fits no existing SaaS model
- The data is a core business asset the company needs to own
- Regulatory requirements the vendor cannot meet
- The tool is the documented bottleneck in a revenue-generating process
