Most writing about how to hire a design studio is written from the buying side. This piece is from the other side. We are a studio. We have been hired, rejected, and asked to bid on projects we should not have bid on.

A creative engineering studio is not a marketing agency with engineering bolted on. It is not a development shop with a designer on retainer. It is a structure where design and development run through the same team, and the work that ships in the browser is the work the designer drew. No handoff document. No Figma-to-build translation step that costs you fidelity at the end.

You probably need one if your project requires both visual craft and production engineering, your budget falls between $30k and $300k, and you do not have senior in-house design and engineering already. If your budget is under $15k, hire a senior freelancer. The overhead a studio carries does not earn its cost at that scale.

Real pricing for boutique creative engineering studios in 2026:

- Marketing site: $20k–$80k (motion complexity and CMS depth move this number)
- Product MVP: $50k–$200k (scope and integration count move this number)
- Design system: $30k–$100k (with or without the React component library)
- Mobile app MVP: $60k–$200k
- Mid-tier agencies charge 2–3x these ranges. Big agencies charge 5–10x.

The red flags that matter during a sales process:
- The founder runs the pitch but is absent after the kickoff
- Pricing is hourly with no cap
- The portfolio is mostly older than 18 months
- The studio cannot explain a single decision in detail, only the result

The signal that separates a serious studio from a competent-but-ordinary one is whether they push back. Senior studios tell you the scope is too big for the timeline. They tell you the brief is unclear and that no execution will save it. A studio that agrees to everything in the first call is selling, not partnering.

Two revision rounds per major deliverable is the industry standard. If a studio promises unlimited revisions, they are either underpricing or quietly limiting through other means.
