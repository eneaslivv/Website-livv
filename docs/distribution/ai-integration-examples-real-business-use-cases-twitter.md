2/ The workflows that reached production shared a profile: single-sentence describable problem, more than 20 distinct inputs per operator per day, visible error rate or cost under the current process. Workflows without this profile failed their PoC.

3/ Legal: contract clause extraction built for $18K-$24K, ~$800/month API costs. Real estate: lease amendment comparison dropped analyst time from 4-6 hours to under 1 hour per amendment. Build cost $14K-$20K.

4/ Insurance claims triage: $22K-$30K build, 300 claims/week at a P&C insurer. Audit evidence classification: $12K-$16K, days of work dropped to hours per engagement. Consulting firm RAG (60 staff, 7 years of docs): $35K-$55K. Citations drove adoption.

5/ What all 10 cases share: no case removed a human from the workflow entirely. AI handled the high-volume mechanical work. Humans retained the judgment calls. No fine-tuning in any case. All used frontier models with prompt engineering and retrieval augmentation.

6/ Real 2026 cost ranges: AI feature on an existing app $8K-$40K. RAG system $20K-$80K. Custom AI agent replacing a defined workflow $30K-$120K. Development cost was recoverable within 12-18 months of production in each case.
