We shipped a multi-locale franchise site in Webflow and a motion-heavy B2B site in Framer in the same quarter. Here is what actually differs between them.

Most Webflow vs Framer comparisons are written by people who have used one tool seriously and the other casually. The tell is the structure: feature checklists, pricing tiers, then "it depends on your needs." Useful to nobody.

We work in both. Our split runs roughly 40 percent Webflow, 50 percent Framer, 10 percent custom Next.js. We have an opinion and we are sharing it here.

Both tools are professional-grade in 2026. They are not interchangeable. The honest comparison is about defaults and second-order trade-offs, not feature lists.

Where Webflow wins:
- Multi-locale at scale: we rebuilt the WMF franchise site in Webflow specifically for this (UK English and US English, structurally divergent content trees, custom locale switcher, per-locale CMS items referencing shared assets)
- Deep CMS relationships: 200+ items nested two to three layers deep, without performance loss
- Enterprise governance: roles, permissions, audit logs, staged publishing environments
- Freelance talent pool: more Webflow developers in 2026 than Framer developers, by roughly an order of magnitude

Where Framer wins:
- Motion and interaction out of the box: the Mobilita site has scroll-velocity parallax, cursor-speed hover states, and viewport-position deceleration. All configuration in Framer. All custom code in Webflow.
- Designer onboarding: a senior designer who has never opened Framer ships a polished page in two days. Webflow takes a week.
- Starting cost: meaningfully cheaper entry point for a single-site launch

Our default at LIVV: Framer for new marketing sites under 50 pages, unless one of three triggers fires. When a trigger fires, Webflow.

The tools cover the marketing site space between them well. Very little is left uncovered. The next time someone asks which one is better, the less dishonest answer is: Framer unless something specific pulls you to Webflow.
